# Escalation Criteria

## Mandatory Escalation Triggers

A case must be escalated, rather than approved or held in review, when
any of the following are true: a confirmed sanctions match, a fraud score
above 0.70, identity verification that fails a second time after
correction, or an EDD case where required documents cannot be obtained.

## Escalation Process

Escalated cases are routed to a compliance analyst with the full case
record, the fraud score and its top contributing features, sanctions
screening results, and the specific policy citations that triggered the
escalation. The agent must not attempt to resolve an escalated case
itself.

## Timelines

Escalated cases must receive an initial analyst response within one
business day. Cases involving a confirmed sanctions match must be
reported per the regulatory filing timeline in effect for the customer's
jurisdiction.
